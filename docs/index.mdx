## Getting Started

To gain the most value out of these guides, we recommend following the steps below:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Learn the Physics">
    Before placing a probe on a patient, understand how high-frequency sound waves travel and
    interact with biological tissues.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Know Your Modes">
    Differentiate when to use standard 2D B-mode, M-mode (for movement over time), and the various
    Doppler modes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Master Knobology">
    Understand how to optimize gain, depth, and time-gain compensation (TGC) to get high-quality
    clinical images.
  </Step>
</Steps>

:::tip
Always use adequate acoustic coupling gel to eliminate the thin air layer between the transducer and the skin. Air has high acoustic impedance, causing 100% reflection of sound waves before they can even enter the tissue!
:::
